Role

I ideated and skectched out ideas for our exhibit. I conducted research on exemplars, and the kind of museum our exhibit would be a part of. Besides that, I co-wrote the voice over and recorded it for the video. While facilitating the team, I contributed all throughout the small assignments we had during the course of the whole project.


Team

Duration

6-8 weeks


Tags

Experience Design


Target Museum, Audience

EMP Museum at Seattle Center, Adults who are new to classical music or are not really into it.

This project was about designing 'an experience'. As per the project prompt, we were to design a museum exhibit which would give our visitor a great experience! It was up to us to choose who we wanted to design for and what would be the designed exhibit about. We studied many theories centered around the different approaches to understand what makes an experience. All those theories gave us pointers. I am happy that we were able to apply some of those theories to our design.

As we are talking about experiences, I cannot tell you the story of 'An Interactive Experience With Classical Music' without briefly reflecting on how my own experience was in this project. The experience had a single pervading quality, which I had of feeling unsure and uncomfortable. The overall felt experience was not good. My be-goals were not completely met. Given to a few reasons, I could feel a few breaks in the flow. Nevertheless, I am sure of this one thing that the parts of this story would make a complete whole. The whole which I am still trying to come out from.

Before we get into the details of this project's design process, lets check out the final design.

While we brainstormed, we realized that we needed a design core (specific design direction) and have to narrow down further. We had strong reasons to design for classical music.

How about Storytelling?

An interview with Brant, who is a music undergraduate. He told us about his own experience with classical music and how he started listening to it. He also shared numerous classical music tracks with us.

Brant Hughes


Gathering insights from our discussions with Brant, we had the first version of our design core. Core - "Changing people's perspective on Classical Music by using Storytelling"


Experience Prototyping - It was time to evaluate our ideas

We wanted to evaluate the approach of using storytelling to provide a way in to classical music. We used an anime video (cartoon series) playing classical music in the background. We blacked out certain parts of the video and asked our users to anticipate those parts of the story by listening to the transitions in the track. We tested our video with two users.




Dan Richert
Adithya R Kumar




A still from our prototype video. '02' secs left before the video goes black.




Results of the Experience Prototype Evaluation:

1) Storytelling was taking away the experience of listening to classical music.
2) May be there was a better way than anticipating parts of a story for people to express themselves while listening to the music.
3) Even when Adhithya and Dan were completely involved watching the video, they mentioned that storytelling would not make them start listening to classical music. Neither would it change their perspective.


We were kind of realizing that storytelling would not work. Our idea failed. We had mixed feelings about it because something bad was rooted out, but at the same time we had to find an alternative way to target our design core.

To expedite the process of uncovering limitations and hidden aspects of our design, we used Pastische Scenario

We chose two fictional characters and wrote their visitor's journey around our exhibit. Our exhibit which would be placed in the music section of the EMP Museum at Seattle Center.



Visitors sitting on chairs with VR sets
Watchig the video on VR sets




A part of the video where the visitor is prompted to express
Visitors could talk to each other after watching the video




Writing pastische scenarios highlighted some of the limitations of our design.

1) The pastiche scenario helped us to pay attention to details that we wouldn't normally think of. For example, what would visitors do when they were waiting? What would family do? Is this good for children?
2) Asking people about how they are feeling in the middle of the video might break their experience?
3) The pastiche scenarios also helped us think from the perspective of the ‘non-ideal’ user. For example, would skeptics or music aficionados have the same experience?




Even when we have designed for expression and reflection around Western Classical Music, it had loopholes. It was becoming a serious block! How could we make sure that people have a good experience while listening to Classical Music! But how could be sure that if they have a good experience, they would start appreciating clasical music or atleast understand its depth. We wanted our visitors to have a good experience by themselves and also with others. We knew reflection or expression is an vital aspect of our design because classical music has transitions. The transitions correspond to various emotional transitions in the story of a musical piece. Ideas around actively involving people were not working out for our design core.

I definitely felt we were missing something. It did not feel good at all. Just like myself, I could feel that my team members were losing interest too! We discussed ideas, but they all felt half baked.

It took a session of some harsh feedback from one of our mentor that made us came up with something new!

We ideated for a concept where a classical music track would be broken down into its parts. Our visitor would have the chance to explore its parts and express himself with all the visitors at the exhibit. It was as simple as it could be. And I like Simple a lot! After some research, we found out about these direction speakers.

What I learned

1) I learned new methods of hashing out details and limitations of a design.
2) Designing an experience for a museum exhibit is a fairly dificult task.
3) I need to be more open to conflicts and confrontations.
4) Critical Design is an interesting design domain. It was not useful to us in this project, but I am going to use it to generate alternatives in future projects.
5) I am thinking about how one could keep up her/his motivation and participation all the time in long duration projects.

This is not an exhaustive list. I would keep adding to this. I will add to this whenever I reflect back to my experience in this project.
